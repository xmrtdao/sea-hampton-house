## First Public Offering in Napeague Camping Club History: Rare Waterfront Cottage Hits the Market at $750,000

**31 Harbor Road, Amagansett, NY — A once-in-a-generation opportunity in the Hamptons' last authentic waterfront community**

---

**AMAGANSETT, NY — June 24, 2026** — For what is believed to be the first time in the 70+ year history of the Napeague Camping Club, a residence within this private waterfront community is being offered to the public market. Located at 31 Harbor Road in Amagansett, New York, the three-bedroom, two-bathroom cottage is listed at **$750,000** — one of the most affordable waterfront properties currently available in the Hamptons.

The Napeague Camping Club, established in the 1950s as a tent campground, evolved over decades into a unique residential community of working waterfront families. Situated on a narrow strip of land between the Atlantic Ocean and Gardiners Bay, Napeague remains one of the least populated and least developed parts of the Hamptons — a deliberate contrast to the luxury estates that dominate the region's real estate narrative.

"This is a rare opportunity to own a piece of authentic Hamptons heritage," said David Elze, the property owner. "Most transfers within the Napeague Camping Club have historically happened privately — within families or among people already in the community. This is the first time the club has allowed a public market sale, and it may well be the last."

### About Napeague Camping Club

The Napeague Camping Club is a not-for-profit community established in the 1950s on a narrow strip of land between the Atlantic Ocean and Gardiners Bay in Amagansett, New York. Originally a tent campground, it evolved into a residential community of working waterfront families. The club is known for its authentic maritime culture, world-class kiteboarding conditions, and unspoiled natural environment. It remains one of the least developed parts of the Hamptons.

### About 31 Harbor Road

31 Harbor Road is a modern three-bedroom, two-bathroom waterfront cottage within the Napeague Camping Club. Offered at $750,000, it represents the first public offering in the club's history and one of the most affordable waterfront opportunities in the Hamptons.
