## Objection Handling

### "It's too small (960 sq ft)"
**Response:**
> "That's exactly what makes it special — and affordable. Most Hamptons waterfront is 3,000-5,000 sqft estates at $3M+. This is for people who want the water, not the maintenance. 960 sq ft is perfect for weekends, easy to heat/cool, and leaves more budget for actually enjoying the Hamptons."

### "Napeague? I've never heard of it."
**Response:**
> "That's the point — it's one of the last undeveloped, authentic parts of the Hamptons. Most people know East Hampton and Montauk. Napeague is the quiet strip between them, where working waterfront families have lived for 70 years. It's for people who want the real Hamptons, not the postcard version."

### "The Camping Club sounds unusual."
**Response:**
> "It's a 70-year-old community, not a campground. Think of it like a co-op or condo association, but with actual land ownership. The 'Camping Club' name is historical — it started as a tent site in the 1950s and evolved into a residential community. You own the property outright, you're just part of a unique community with shared waterfront access."

### "Why is this the first public offering?"
**Response:**
> "Great question. Most transfers happened privately within families or to people already in the community. This is the first time the club has allowed a public market sale. That's why it's such a rare opportunity — and likely the last. Once this sells, the club may return to private-only transfers."

### "I'm worried about resale value."
**Response:**
> "Understandable. But scarcity drives value — they're not making any more Napeague. There's a fixed number of properties here, and this is the first one to hit the open market in decades. Plus, at $750K, you're well below replacement cost. If you wanted to buy the land and build a similar cottage today, you'd be at $1M+ easily."

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk: No Showings in First 3 Weeks
**Mitigation:**
- Reduce price to $725K immediately
- Increase Dan's Papers ad size/frequency
- Hire local real estate photographer for fresh photos
- Pay for Zillow "Premier Agent" boost

### Risk: Lowball Offers (<$650K)
**Mitigation:**
- Hold firm at $750K, counter at $735K
- Remind buyers of comps ($825K Montauk cottage with no water)
- Emphasize uniqueness (first public offering, scarcity)
- Be willing to walk away — property is not distressed

### Risk: Financing Falls Through
**Mitigation:**
- Require pre-approval letter before showing
- Prefer cash or strong conventional financing (20%+ down)
- Short attorney review period (7 days)
- Substantial earnest money ($50K+)

### Risk: Market Downturn
**Mitigation:**
- Price aggressively from start ($750K is already below comps)
- Be prepared to hold and rent ($3-5K/week in summer)
- Unique properties hold value better than generic stock
